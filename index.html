The Simple Version

Think of it like a home inspection — but for a company's technology.

When you buy a house, you hire an inspector to check the roof, plumbing, electrical, and foundation before signing. Investment firms do the same thing when buying companies — except instead of pipes and wiring, I inspect the software, the engineering team, the servers, and the security. My report tells the buyer what's solid, what needs fixing, and how much it will cost.

These investment firms — called Private Equity (PE) funds — buy companies worth tens or hundreds of millions of euros. A bad technology decision could cost them millions. They need an independent expert who can cut through the sales pitch and give them the truth.

How a Typical Engagement Works

Each project takes 10–15 working days. Here's the process:

1

The Call

An investment firm is about to buy a company. They call me and say: «We need someone to check their technology before we sign.»

2

The Investigation

I review the company's code, interview the tech team, analyze their infrastructure, check their security, and look at their costs. Deep, structured, methodical.

3

The Report

I deliver a detailed report: what's strong, what's risky, exactly how much it would cost to fix, and a roadmap for improvements. Numbers, not opinions.

4

The Decision

The investor uses my report to decide: buy, negotiate a lower price, or walk away. My work directly protects millions in investment.

The 7 Things I Check

My framework covers every angle. Tap any item to see what it means in plain terms.

🏗
Architecture

Like checking a building's foundations and load-bearing walls. Is the software built on solid ground? Can it handle 10x more users without collapsing? How much «technical debt» — deferred maintenance — has built up, and what would it cost to fix?

👥
Team & People

Who built this, and will they stay? Is there one genius holding everything together who might leave? Does the team have the skills needed for what comes next? Are they well-organized or chaotic?

🔒
Security

Could a hacker break in? Is customer data properly protected? Does the company comply with European privacy laws (GDPR)? One breach after acquisition could cost more than the technology itself.

📊
Data & Analytics

Is the company's data clean and usable, or a mess? Can they actually measure what's happening in their business? Is their data ready for AI and advanced analytics — the things that create future value?

💰
Costs

Is the company spending too much on technology? Are they overpaying for cloud services, software licenses, or staff? I quantify exactly where money is being wasted and how much could be saved.

⚙️
Delivery Speed

How fast can this team ship new features? Are they releasing updates daily, monthly, or struggling to release at all? A slow team means slow growth — and growth is what the investor is paying for.

🎯
Strategic Fit

Does the technology actually support where the business is going? If they want to expand into new markets or double in size, can the tech handle it — or will it need to be rebuilt from scratch?

Who Hires Me

My clients are investment firms that buy and grow companies for a living.

Private Equity Funds

Firms that buy companies, improve them over 3-7 years, and sell them for a profit. They manage billions of euros from pension funds, universities, and wealthy families. Before buying, they need to know what they're getting — that's where I come in.

The Sweet Spot

Companies with EUR 30M–400M in revenue. Big enough to have real technology, small enough that a single expert can assess it in two weeks. Too small for the big consulting firms, too important for guesswork.
